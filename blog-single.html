About Cancer: Types, Symptoms and Treatment In Kenya

Facts and Life Hacks, Health and Wellness Not a day goes by without us hearing of 'cancer' and the harm it causes on humans. Cancer is among the leading causes of death worldwide, causing more deaths than HIV, TB, and Malaria combined. Clearly, it is a very risky condition that we need to be aware of. This detailed piece of writing gives information on the types, symptoms, and treatment of different cancers in Kenya. cancer in kenya Image: canva.com (modified by author) Source: Original Morbidity and mortality due to cancer rank third after infectious and cardiovascular diseases in Kenya. According to medical health statistics, there is about 39,000 new incidences per annum and approximately 27,000 national mortality every year. One of the biggest challenges we face as a country is diagnosis which in most cases is done late when the patient is already on the third or fourth phases of the disease. Additionally, the country has a few oncologists resulting into trial and error procedures by most practitioners. Lucky for us there are new technologies in Kenya that will spearhead combating the cancer menace. The following article will explore the various types of cancers prevalent in Kenya, their symptoms, and some of the available treatment options and costs in the country.

Cancer Prevention

Even though, there is no certain way to prevent one from contracting this deadly disease, oncologists worldwide have identified a number of ways to reduce your incidence risk. Stop smoking as it is not only linked to lung cancer but also other forms of cancer Always eat a well-balanced diet rich in all the nutrients Avoid excessive sun exposure by wearing protective clothes, using sun screen or staying in a shade. Ensure to exercise at least 30 minutes or more for most days of the week. Keep your weight stable as being overweight may heighten your risk of cancer. Consume alcoholic drinks in moderation or alleviate them completely. Ensure to get immunizations that prevent you from specific virus that augment the risk of cancer such as the human papillomavirus (HPV). .

Cancer Treatment

Kenya still has fewer oncologists who are mostly found in a few health facilities in Nairobi. This means that Kenyans in remote areas have to travel longer distances and when they get to the city they are subjected to long queues. As such, this causes previously curable tumours to advance to incurable stages and it has been reported that some patients even die while still awaiting treatment. Cancer is treated through medical, surgical, and radiation therapies such as chemotherapy to kill the cancerous cells and prevent them from spreading. Some of the best cancer hospitals in Kenya are situated in Nairobi county and the include; Texas Cancer Centre, Cancer Care Kenya, Aga Khan University Hospital, Beacon Health Services Cancer Treatment Center, The Nairobi Hospital Cancer Treatment Centre, Kenyatta National Hospital, and Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital found in AMPATH Oncology Center. The most affordable cancer treatment in Kenya costs Ksh 500,000 and this is not even cheap for the ordinary Kenyan. This figure can go as high as millions of Kenyan sillings when blood cancer is involved. Among the barriers limiting palliative care in Kenya includes shortage of oncologist, lack of finances, scarce medications used in pain management especially in the public hospitals, lack of awareness of importance cancer screenings and legal limitations of the use and availability of opioid analgesics. .
