DECIDES PROJECT - Early Detection Saves lives

More than 90% of high-income countries reported treatment services are available compared to less than 30% of low-income countries. The economic impact of cancer is significant and is increasing. The total annual economic cost of cancer in 2010 was estimated at approximately US$ 1.16 trillion . Only 1 in 5 low- and middle-income countries have the necessary data to drive cancer policy . Cancer infections have cleared many family lifelong savings and investments, pushing them to extreme poverty. the project enhances the recognizing and possible warning signs of cancer and taking prompt action leading to early diagnosis and treatment. this saves families from pain, high costs and leverage household incomes.Tackling this challenge is important from both a humanitarian and an economic perspective.In Kenya, most people believe cancer is a punishment for a sin or the result of witchcraft, a cancer diagnosis is far more stigmatizing than HIV.

FREE MEDICAL CAMPS

The increased disease burden in the low income settings has an implication on household incomes thus limiting access to appropriate health care. The medical camp will provide an opportunity for the under-privileged people to access free treatment and health education with an aim of improving their health. The main objective of the camp is to create awareness on the risk of acquiring non- communicable diseases such as High blood pressure, diabetes and obesity and screen for Breast, cervical and Ovarian cancers.

July 26, 2018

Screening and Diagnosis

Vesh Foundation has developed partnership with health providers who volunteer Medical personnel to conduct Screening exercises. Vesh Foundation mobilize support from Friends to hire tents, Purchase Screening Kits ad provide logistical support to volunteers. The excersise are provided free to hundred of poor women who struggle in may ways to access basic life needs as water, food and help own children go to school. .

Supporting Early treatment

Vesh Foundation supports positive cases through counselling, provide support and registration to the National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF), the project assist the women through support groups start small income generation activities to sustainable contribution to NHIF. NHIF meets 85% of public and private medical bills. .
